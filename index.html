Background

Understanding the Conflict

The Gaza Strip, a 41-kilometer long and 6-12 kilometer wide territory along the Mediterranean coast, has been at the center of the Israeli-Palestinian conflict for decades. Home to over 2 million Palestinians, it has faced a blockade since 2007.

The current escalation began on October 7, 2023, marking a significant turning point in the ongoing conflict.

Humanitarian

Crisis Overview

The conflict has created one of the world's most severe humanitarian emergencies, with widespread displacement, infrastructure damage, and limited access to essential services.

International

Global Response

The international community has called for ceasefires, humanitarian corridors, and diplomatic solutions while providing aid and engaging in ongoing negotiations.

Historical Timeline

Key events that shaped the Gaza conflict

1948

The Nakba

Approximately 700,000 Palestinians were displaced during the 1948 Arab-Israeli War. Many refugees fled to Gaza, dramatically increasing its population.
1967

Six-Day War

Israel captured the Gaza Strip from Egypt during the Six-Day War, beginning a military occupation that would last for decades.
1987

First Intifada

A Palestinian uprising began in Gaza before spreading to the West Bank, lasting until 1993 and the Oslo Accords.
2005

Israeli Disengagement

Israel unilaterally withdrew its military forces and settlers from Gaza, though maintained control over borders, airspace, and coastal waters.
2007

Blockade Begins

Following Hamas's takeover of Gaza, Israel and Egypt imposed a blockade that severely restricted the movement of people and goods.
2008-2009

Operation Cast Lead

A 22-day Israeli military offensive launched in response to rocket fire. The conflict resulted in approximately 1,400 Palestinian deaths and 13 Israeli deaths, with significant damage to Gaza's infrastructure including hospitals and schools.
2014

Operation Protective Edge

A 50-day conflict that became the deadliest Gaza war at the time. Over 2,200 Palestinians and 73 Israelis were killed. The UN estimated 100,000 Gazans were left homeless, with over 18,000 housing units destroyed or severely damaged.
2023

October 7 Attack & Response

Hamas launched a large-scale attack on southern Israel. Israel responded with a military campaign in Gaza that continues to the present day.

Understanding the Context

Key factors and perspectives in the conflict

Economic Impact

The blockade has severely impacted Gaza's economy, with unemployment rates exceeding 45% and limited access to international markets. Essential supplies including fuel, medicine, and construction materials face significant restrictions.

Demographics

Gaza is one of the most densely populated areas in the world. Nearly 70% of its population are refugees or descendants of refugees from the 1948 war, living in eight refugee camps.

International Law

The situation in Gaza involves complex questions of international humanitarian law, including debates about occupation, blockade legality, and the protection of civilians in armed conflict.

Healthcare

Gaza's healthcare system has been under immense pressure, with hospitals facing shortages of medicine, equipment, and fuel for generators. The ongoing conflict has made medical access increasingly difficult.

Education

Despite challenges, Gaza has maintained high literacy rates. However, schools have been damaged or used as shelters, and the psychological impact on children remains a serious concern.

Displacement

The current conflict has caused unprecedented internal displacement, with the majority of Gaza's population forced to flee their homes, often multiple times.
